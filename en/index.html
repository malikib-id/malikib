People also ask

What is Automated Testing, and why does my business need it?

Automated Testing is a website testing service that utilizes specialized code scripts to test your features automatically, quickly, and instantly without the risk of human error. You need it to ensure crucial workflows (such as payment processing or checkout) do not suddenly fail when accessed by customers, thereby protecting your revenue and brand reputation.

Is the testing performed directly on my live production website?

For user data security and the smooth operation of your business, testing must be conducted in a staging or development environment (a replica of your official website). I do not recommend testing directly on the live production site, as it risks disrupting active users.

How is the service pricing determined?

My pricing is highly flexible to suit your budget. Since we will be collaborating through Upwork, we can work with an hourly rate (ranging from $15 to $25/hr) for ongoing support, or a fixed-price contract tailored specifically to the number of user flows and the complexity of the test automation scenarios you need.

Why does your service use Automated Testing? What is the benefit for my business?

Automated Testing uses specialized code scripts to test your website automatically, instantly, and with minimal risk of human error. This service is highly beneficial because the scripts can be executed repeatedly every time your developers deploy code updates, saving you time and money on releasing new features in the long run.

What is your testing workflow and warranty policy?

My workflow focuses on stabilizing your feature flows before any automation scripts are written:

  1. Flow Validation Phase: I perform manual exploratory testing to ensure your feature flows are finalized and free of critical errors. If any issues arise, I will report them to your developer and provide re-testing warranties based on our mutual agreement once the fixes are deployed.
  2. Script Generation Phase: Once the feature flow is proven stable (meaning business logic and page structures won't shift significantly soon), I will begin writing the automated test scripts.
Why must the feature workflow be stable before scripts are created?

Automation scripts follow precise, step-by-step actions on your website. If your development team makes significant workflow adjustments mid-way (such as changing button structures or skipping process pages), the automation scripts being built can break. Ensuring a stable workflow from the start guarantees that your automation scripts remain accurate and durable.
